I quote. â€œIn the wake of the Black Lives Matter movement, calls for meaningful reform to policing and efforts to change the criminal justice system are underway. In the United States, police play an important role in societal governance. Unfortunately, the structural power that has been bestowed upon them enables police to target and abuse groups who traditionally have less social-economic status and political standing. The dynamic between the police and the people they are supposed to protect has led to law enforcement's wide-spread mistreatment of individuals, mainly people from marginalized populations, people of color, people with non-heteronormative sexual identity, and sex workers. The foregoing abuse is compounded and its effects are amplified if the targeted individual is a member of more than one marginalized group, such as a Black transgendered sex worker. Police officers abusing their power has been well documented, and not just through complaints of excessive force. Police officers, especially those on vice squads, have a lengthy history of complaints by citizens and investigations by internal affairs for all manner of crime: drug abuse, gun trafficking, rape, excessive force, etc. Often, when police misconduct is reported, police departments do not take these accusations of excessive force, sexual misconduct or any other type of misconduct seriously.'"
